A Gallery of Mental Health Themes Through Art

Vincent Van Gogh (1853-1890)

Van Gogh suffered from anxiety and depression throughout his short life, and he once wrote, “I put my heart and my soul into my work, and lost my mind in the process.”

See More

Edgar Degas (1834-1917)

DeIn the 1880s, Degas suffered from bouts of depression and aimlessness. “I’m blocked, impotent. I’ve lost the thread,” he wrote in a letter in 1884.

See More

Edvard Munch (1863-1944)

Norwegian artist Edvard Munch suffered from anxiety and hallucinations. The painter created his most famous image, The Scream, after it came to him when he was out for a walk at sunset on a fjord overlooking Oslo.

See More

Mark Rothko (1903-1970)

Rothko suffered from bouts of depression and was a heavy drinker. Viewing the vibrating colors in a large-scale Rothko painting in person at a museum is almost a mystical, meditative experience that should not be missed.

See More

Georgia O’Keeffe (1887-1986)

The American artist Georgia O’Keeffe suffered from depression, although it’s possible her depression was situational. She was admitted to Doctors Hospital in New York City for treatment after a nervous breakdown in 1933.

See More

Michelangelo Buonarroti (1475-1564)

historians speculate that Michelangelo had obsessive-compulsive disorder. He also suffered from depression and anxiety, shutting himself away from the world for days at a time to work

Navigating Mental Health Consultation Commonly Asked Questions

A mental health consultant is a professional who provides support, guidance, and resources to individuals seeking to improve their mental health and well-being. They offer personalized assistance tailored to each individual's needs and goals.
We offer services such as self-assessment tests for self-awareness, connecting you with therapists near you for professional support, providing articles and resources for education and guidance, and offering chat support for immediate assistance and Art related to different mental health issues.
Working with a mental health consultant can benefit you by providing personalized support, coping strategies, and insights to improve your mental well-being and overall quality of life.
